Privacy Act 2024

§1 Purpose & Scope

§1.1 This Act regulates the processing of personal data by controllers and processors operating within the jurisdiction.

§1.2(a) Personal data shall be processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently.
§1.2(b) Processing must be limited to the stated, explicit purposes.

§2 Lawful Bases

§2.1 A controller shall establish at least one lawful basis: consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, or legitimate interests.

§2.2 Explicit consent is required for special-category data unless an exemption applies.

§3 Cross-Border Transfers

§3.1 A controller shall not transfer personal data to a third country unless:
(a) an adequacy decision exists; or
(b) appropriate safeguards are in place (including standard contractual clauses); or
(c) the data subject has provided explicit consent after being informed of risks.

§3.2 Occasional, necessary transfers for contract performance are permitted where proportionate and documented.

§4 Data Subject Rights

§4.1 Individuals have rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection.

§5 Retention

§5.1 Data shall be retained only as long as necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, unless a longer period is required by law.

§6 Breach Notification

§6.1 Controllers shall notify the Authority of a personal data breach without undue delay and, where feasible, within 72 hours after becoming aware of it.
